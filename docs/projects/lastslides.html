Predictability

contents

Covid was and will still be a big deal going forward. Did anyone predict that before 2019? The answer is yes and no.

To my knowledge, Bill Gates has been worried about this sort of crisis for quite some time.

Perhaps a practical option for the conundrum would be estimate the global impact and multiply by the likelyhood of it happening over a given peroid of time.

...right?

Yes and no.

It'd work for known problems with known solutions. It doesn't work for things like climate change or existential threats.

Climate change is a known problem and does have solutions but difficulty lies in the predictability of the weather. If no-one can measure the impact a particular solution has, how on earth would you know which one to pick?

Killing Creativity

If kids don't know, they'll give it a go. Going through the process of education usually stops that.

If you're not prepared to be wrong, you'll never come up with anything original.

What is the product and service of education? University professors and academic ability.

Public education was designed to meet the needs of industrialism.

Degrees aren't worth much anymore.

Children's capcity for innovation

We know 3 things about intellegence:

1 - It's diverse. IQ is a one dimensional measurement of a multi-dimensional space.

2 - It's dynamic. Having original idea's that have value prodominally come about through remixing.

3 - It's distinct. For some very intellegent people moving is thinking. Calling that a learning disorder and giving them medication to calm down is a cop-out.

Adopting a new conception of human ecology where we reconstitue our conception of the richness of human capcity. Our current system of education has mined our minds in the same way we've strip mined the earth for a particular commodity. For the future, that system won't service. We need to rethink the fundamental principles upon which we educate our children.

What's the job of education? It's educating the whole being of our children for a future we will never see so that they can make something of it.

Fast forward a couple of conversations and I now only see those complaints by women as an unresolvable conflict.

To bring it all together, we need only to watch the fabulous fairy god mother scene from Cinderella (2021).

When Cinderella meets her fairy god mother and is transformed into a princess, she then asks why the kneels are so umcomfortable and impossible to walk in.

God Mother: "Women's shoes are as they are. Even magic has it's limits"

Honestly, I think that's fashion saying to women we've had enough.

While women want to look attractive to their partner, there are limits. You can conceal a pocket within womens clothing easily enough but putting anything in that pocket creates a bulge. Women are concious enough already about different bulges, so do you really think the most attractive clothing will allow you to create one?
